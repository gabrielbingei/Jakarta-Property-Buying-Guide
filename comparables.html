You're being offered half-price square metres. Here's why — and who else is offering them.

At Rp 2–2.5bn for 120sqm and Rp 3–3.5bn for 170–180sqm, Somerset Berlian is trading around IDR 17–21 million per square metre. Two hundred metres away, Pakubuwono View asks 33–42 million. That gap is the entire decision. This sheet is about what it buys, what it costs you, and the two buildings that beat Somerset at its own game.

01  /  The reframe

"Forever" flips three of the five criteria

Most apartment advice — including the general guide on the previous sheet — is written for people who will sell. You won't. That changes what matters.

  • Resale liquidityStops mattering. Somerset's biggest weakness in an investment frame — thin resale, 1990s stock, no MRT — is a weakness you are choosing not to care about. Fine. It also means you should pay the discount price and not a rupiah more.
  • Price growthStops mattering. You're not harvesting appreciation. Space, light, quiet and management quality are what you consume for thirty years.
  • HGB expiryBecomes the single most important line on the certificate. A 30-year building right on a building completed around 1993 has already had, or is about to have, its first renewal. Forever is longer than a strata title's default runway. This is the one thing that can turn a good buy into a disaster.
  • Sinking fundBecomes existential. In year 12 the lifts, chillers and pumps need replacing. In a 104-unit building, your share of that bill is 1/104. In a 384-unit building it is 1/384. The maths is unkind to small towers.
  • ManagementBecomes the whole product. You've already noticed Somerset's maintenance is good. That is not an accident — it is professionally operated as a serviced residence. Understand what happens if that operator ever leaves.

How to read the prices below. These are asking prices scraped from live listings in mid-2026, not transaction prices. Indonesian listing data is noisy — I found at least one obviously mispriced entry while compiling this. Treat every figure as a bracket to negotiate inside, and remember the market median discount to asking is around 4%, with large old units typically going further because the buyer pool for them is thin.

The headline finding. Somerset is not the cheap outlier you might think. Before you decide whether Somerset is good value, you need to know it has a real competitor in its own price band. It does.

03  /  Same money, same neighbourhood

The two buildings that beat Somerset at its own game

These are the comparables that actually threaten the purchase — not because they're grander, but because they offer the same thing for the same money, and in one case for less.

The bet you're making: that a low per-sqm entry price in a genuinely leafy, prestigious pocket, in a professionally managed building, beats a newer building at double the price. For a forever home, that is a defensible bet. But you must verify the HGB and the sinking fund first — see section 09.

Why it matters: this is the same product at a lower per-sqm price, essentially next door, on the Simprug side of Permata Hijau — arguably a quieter and more prestigious pocket than Grogol Utara, adjacent to Simprug Golf and Pakubuwono. Units run genuinely large (175, 185, 195, 250sqm), 3–4BR with maid's quarters, and it is pet-friendly, which most Jakarta towers are not.

04  /  What double the money buys

The step-up tier — and whether it's worth it

At 33–42m/sqm you are paying roughly twice Somerset's rate. The honest question is not "is it nicer" (it is) but "what am I buying that I will still value in year twenty-five?"

The Pakubuwono View

Jl. Sultan Iskandar Muda No. 12, Kebayoran Lama · 2010 · HGB
The default upgrade
Asking34–42m/sqm
Units in strata384
149sqm 3BR~Rp 5.0bn
180sqm 3BR~Rp 7.6bn

What the premium buys, concretely: a 2010 building instead of a 1993 one — roughly 17 more years of HGB runway and 17 fewer years of deferred maintenance. Private lift straight into the unit. Five-star-hotel-grade facilities: a 50m lap pool, indoor heated pool, sky gym, sky lounge, spa, library, clinic, game room, tennis, squash, badminton, basketball. Two parking slots as standard on larger units. 384 owners to share every capital repair with, not 104.

What it doesn't buy: a better location. It is on Sultan Iskandar Muda, a few minutes from Permata Hijau, in the same Kebayoran Lama. It has no MRT either.

The verdict: if the extra Rp 2.5–4bn is money you'd otherwise leave in the bank, this is the rational forever choice — the HGB runway and the 384-owner sinking fund alone are worth serious money over thirty years. If it means stretching the mortgage, it is not.

06  /  The comparable nobody shows you

At Rp 3–3.5bn, a house is on the table

This is the most important paragraph on this page, and no agent will raise it, because agents are paid on the transaction in front of them.

For a forever home, the strongest argument against every building above is that none of them gives you land. An apartment on HGB is a wasting asset with a renewal clock. A house on SHM (Hak Milik) is perpetual, inheritable, and the land under it is the only part of Indonesian residential property that reliably appreciates — roughly 3% a year against apartments' ~1%.

Your Rp 3–3.5bn budget, which buys 170–180sqm of 1990s strata in Permata Hijau, will also buy a real house on real freehold land in Cipulir, Kebayoran Lama proper, Bintaro, Cirendeu, Pondok Pinang or Bintaro Jaya — further out, more maintenance, more security to arrange, but yours in perpetuity and your children's after that.

The honest trade

  • Apartment wins onSecurity you don't manage. Facilities. Zero maintenance. Central location. Instant liveability. Community. No gardener, no leaking roof, no flood at your front door. For a couple without children, and for people who travel, this is a genuinely large quality-of-life win and it is rational to pay for it.
  • House wins onPerpetual title. Land appreciation. Space and privacy. No service charge, no sinking fund, no strata politics, no dependence on someone else's management competence for thirty years. And nobody can vote to spend your money.
  • The tiebreakHow much do you two value not managing a building? Be honest. If the answer is "a great deal," buy the apartment and stop reading. If the answer is "we could live with it," you owe yourselves one weekend looking at houses at the same price before you sign anything.

One more thing on "forever". Ask yourselves the uncomfortable question: at 70, do you want stairs, a garden and a gate — or a lift, a lobby and a guard? Apartments age with people better than houses do. That is a real argument and it may be the deciding one. Just make it deliberately.

The "cost per sqm lived in" divides each option's total by its floor area — the fairest single number for a forever home, because it prices the space you actually get, not the ticket.

Why the escalation slider matters more than the price field. A service charge rising 5% a year triples over thirty years. On 175sqm, a charge going from Rp 3.85m/month to Rp 16m/month is not a rounding error — it is a second mortgage. Ask both buildings for their five-year history of IPL increases, not just today's rate. A building that has held its charge flat is telling you either that it is superbly run, or that it is deferring maintenance. Find out which.

09  /  Before you commit

Six questions. Ask them of every building on this list.

Not "what's the view." These. Any building that can't answer them in writing is telling you something.

  1. When does the underlying HGB expire, and who pays to renew it?The decisive question for a forever purchase, and the one most likely to be waved away. On a 1993 building, the first 30-year term has already run. Get the certificate. Read the date. Ask whether it is HGB over state land or HGB over HPL — because renewal over HPL is not automatic and depends on a third party. If nobody can answer this clearly, walk away. This is not negotiable and it is not paranoia.
  2. Show me five years of IPL history and the sinking fund balance.Today's service charge tells you nothing. The trend tells you everything. And in a 104-unit building, ask specifically: when were the lifts, chillers, pumps and generator last replaced, what do they cost, and is there money set aside? Divide by 104. That's your number.
  3. Who actually runs this building, and what happens if they leave?Somerset's maintenance is good because it is professionally operated as a serviced residence. That's a real asset — but it is a contract, not a property of the concrete. Ask what the management agreement is, how long it runs, and what the strata association's plan is if the operator exits. A well-run building that loses its operator becomes an ordinary one very fast.
  1. What is the owner-occupier versus rental-pool split?In a serviced residence, many units may be in a short-let pool. That affects the neighbours you'll have for thirty years, the wear on the common areas, and how the strata association votes. A building of long-term owners behaves completely differently from a building of transient tenants. Ask for the ratio.
  2. How many units are actually occupied?Walk the corridors after dark and count lit windows. Do it at Somerset, Simprug Terrace and Simprug Indah on the same evening. A half-empty tower has a broken service-charge model and, over thirty years, that becomes your problem.
  3. Is the certificate clean?Have your own notary/PPAT run a pengecekan sertifikat at the BPN before any deposit moves: registered owner matches the seller, area matches, no mortgage (hak tanggungan), no court block. Cheap. Non-optional.

10  /  Verdict

What I'd actually do

The case for Somerset, put fairly

You are buying 170–180 square metres of family space in a genuinely leafy, prestigious pocket of South Jakarta, in a professionally maintained building, for the price of a small apartment in the CBD. Per square metre, that is one of the better deals available in prime-adjacent Jakarta. Large family stock in established districts is structurally scarce — consultants say so, and the rental market confirms it. If you never sell, the two things everyone marks Somerset down for (thin resale, no MRT) simply do not bill you.

That is a real, coherent, defensible purchase. It is not a mistake.

The two things I would not skip

1. Go and see Simprug Terrace, this month. It is the same product, five minutes away, asking less per square metre, with bigger units, and it is pet-friendly. You may hate it. But you cannot honestly conclude Somerset is good value without having stood in the rival that is cheaper. This is one afternoon, and it is the highest-leverage afternoon in the whole process.

2. Get the HGB answer in writing before you fall in love. Not from the agent. From the certificate and your own notary. On a building of this vintage, buying "forever" without knowing the expiry and the renewal path is not a risk — it is a guess.

The sequence. One: pull the certificate on the specific Somerset unit and confirm the HGB. Two: request the IPL history and sinking-fund balance in writing. Three: spend one Saturday walking Somerset, Simprug Terrace and Simprug Indah back to back — same day, same weather, same mood. Four: if Somerset still wins after all that, negotiate hard (the discount to asking on large old units is wider than the market's 4% median, because the buyer pool is small) and buy it without a second thought. You'll have earned the confidence.

Method & caveats

Prices are asking prices compiled from live Indonesian listing portals (Rumah123, Pinhome, Coldwell Banker Indonesia, Jendela360, agent listings) in mid-2026, cross-checked against the market research in the main guide. They are not transaction prices and not appraisals. Listing data in Indonesia is noisy — at least one entry encountered while compiling this was clearly mispriced — so every figure here is a bracket, not a fact. Building ages, unit counts and facility lists come from developer and portal descriptions and should be verified on site.

Nothing here is legal, tax or investment advice, and I am not a licensed advisor. Verify title with your own notary/PPAT and the BPN land office before committing any funds.
